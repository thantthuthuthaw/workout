Each exercise 40 times at least.

Jumping Jacks

Tips:
1. Stand upright with your legs together, arms at your sides.
2. Bend your knees slightly, and jump into the air.
3. As you jump, spread your legs to be about shoulder-width apart. Stretch your arms out and over your head.
4. Jump back to starting position and repeat.

Scissor Skier

Tips:
1. Stand tall with your feet together, arms by your sides, and your palms facing your hips.
2. Jump up, bring your right leg forward, your left leg back, and reach your left arm up and your right arm back.
3. Jump again and switch your legs and arms.
4. Continue switching until the set is complete.

Butt Kick

Tips:
Keep the movement slow, until you’ve perfected it, and kick your feet as high as possible.

Rotate Jump Squat

Tips:
1. Start in squat position, jump up as high as possible, and then rotate to the land facing the opposite way.
2. Repeat.

Lunge Jumps

Tips:
1. Take a large step backward and lower your hips, so that your back knee is just above the floor, and your front thigh is parallel to the floor.
2. Jump into the air and switch leg positions.
3. Jump again and return to the starting position.
4. Repeat the exercise until set is complete.

Plank With Alternate Arm & Leg Raise

Tips:
1. Come into plank position with your arms and legs straight and your shoulders above your wrists.
2. With control, raise your right arm up as you simultaneously raise your left leg up off of the ground. Be sure not to rotate at your hip or upper body. Hold for one second.
3. With control, lower your right arm and left leg back to the starting position. That counts as one rep. Repeat with the left arm and right leg.

Side Crunch |Please do the both sides.

Tips:
1. Lie on your back with your hands supporting your head, bend the knees, and rotate your hips to the left.
2. Lift your shoulders off the floor, squeeze the obliques and hold for 1 to 2 seconds.
3. Slowly return to the starting position, repeat, and switch sides.

Side Plank Hip Lift |Please do the both sides.

Tips:
1. Lie on your left side propped up on your left elbow and forearm, shoulders stacked over your elbow, legs stacked on top of each other, and hold a dumbbell in place on top of your right hip.
2. Raise your hips so that your body forms a straight line from head to heels. This is the starting position.
3. Keeping your core braced and your glutes engaged, slowly lower your left hip, tapping it gently on the floor.
4. Reverse the move, returning to side plank position.
5. Repeat for reps, then switch sides, performing equal reps on each.

1 Arm – 1 Leg Crunch |Please do the both sides.

Tips:
1. Start lying on your back with legs together and arms overhead on the ground.
2. Sit up by bringing your shoulders high off the floor and contracting your abs.
3. Reach with one arm towards your rising opposite leg.
4. Lower back to the start position in a controlled manner.
5. Repeat and switch sides at the half way point.

Bicycle Crunch

Tips:
1. Start lying on your back with bent knees and your hands behind your head.
2. Lift your head, neck and shoulders off the ground by contracting your abdominal muscles. At the same time, lift one bent knee up until your thigh is perpendicular to the floor.
3. Extend your leg as you slowly lower your upper body back down, then repeat.

Plank

Tips:
1. Begin on all fours, placing your hands on the ground directly beneath your shoulders.
2. Next, step your feet behind you, grounding down into your toes to form a straight line from head to toe.
3. Engage your legs, glutes, and core as you maintain a neutral neck and spine.
4. Hold for 60 seconds with good form.

Do this for 4 routines.

After one routine, rest 1 minute.
